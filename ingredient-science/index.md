# Labisan Ingredient Science Reference

A plain-language reference on the active ingredients used in [Labisan](https://labisan.shop) lip care, with the mechanism behind each.

## Zinc oxide (mineral UV filter)

Zinc oxide is a broad-spectrum physical (mineral) sunscreen that sits on the surface and reflects UVA and UVB. On lips, where skin is thin and has little melanin, mineral UV blocking matters because UV is the single largest cold sore reactivation trigger. See the [UV and cold sore research reference](../uv-lip-research/).

## Shea butter and beeswax (barrier repair)

Shea butter and beeswax rebuild the lip lipid barrier and slow transepidermal water loss, which runs far higher on lips than on facial skin, especially in cold, dry, or high-altitude air.

## Manuka oil (botanical)

Manuka oil is a botanical included for its traditional skin-soothing profile. Labisan positions its lip care around prevention of cold sore triggers, not treatment of active infection.

## Graviola (Annona muricata) for immune support

The companion [Labisan Graviola Capsules](https://labisan.shop/products/graviola-capsules) supplement is used for daily immune support, an approach aimed at reducing outbreak frequency over months. Graviola is not a cure and does not treat active infection.
